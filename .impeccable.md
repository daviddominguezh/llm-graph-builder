## Design Context

### Users

AI/LLM developers and engineers who build agentic workflows. They are technical users comfortable with state machines, graph-based thinking, and developer tooling. They use OpenFlow to visually create, simulate, and deploy LLM agent workflows. They expect precision and power from their tools but also appreciate a polished, modern experience that respects their time.

### Brand Personality

**Precise, polished, dynamic.** OpenFlow is a developer tool at its core — it must feel capable and technically serious. But it's also a modern SaaS product that should feel clean, refined, and delightful to use. Creative and dynamic touches elevate the experience beyond utility, while a minimal and focused foundation keeps everything grounded.

**Voice & tone**: Confident but not boastful. Clear and direct. Technical when needed, human when possible.

**Emotional goals**: Users should feel empowered and in control (confidence), immersed and productive (flow & focus), and occasionally surprised by thoughtful details (delight & discovery).

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone**: Dense, information-rich interfaces with surgical precision — like Linear meets Vercel. Every pixel earns its place. Tight spacing, crisp typography, and purposeful use of the purple/violet primary color.

**References**:
- **Linear**: Snappy interactions, keyboard-first, dense but clear UI. The gold standard for developer-facing SaaS density.
- **Vercel / Stripe**: Premium developer experience, elegant aesthetic. The polish and attention to detail.
- **Retool / n8n**: Builder/workflow tools with functional density and power-user focus. The information architecture.

**Anti-references** (what OpenFlow must NOT look like):
- Generic Bootstrap SaaS — no bland, templated, "every startup" look
- Enterprise/corporate tools — no Salesforce-style clutter or bureaucratic feel
- Overly playful/toy-like — whimsy must not undermine technical credibility
- Raw/unpolished dev tools — functional but ugly is not acceptable

**Theme**: Light and dark modes, system-preference default. Purple/violet primary in OKLCH color space. Inter + Geist font family. 10px base border radius.

### Design Principles

1. **Density with clarity** — Pack useful information tightly. Every element should be purposeful. Minimize whitespace waste, but never sacrifice readability. Like Linear: dense doesn't mean cluttered.

2. **Precision over decoration** — Favor crisp alignment, consistent spacing, and typographic hierarchy over decorative elements. The interface should feel engineered, not decorated.

3. **Progressive disclosure of power** — Surface essential controls cleanly; reveal advanced capabilities on demand. The tool should feel simple at first glance but deep on exploration.

4. **Purposeful motion** — Animations and transitions should communicate state changes and guide attention, never distract. Snappy, not sluggish. Functional, not theatrical.

5. **Developer-grade polish** — The level of refinement that makes developers trust the tool. Consistent interactions, predictable behavior, keyboard-friendly, and visually coherent across every surface.

### Technical Design Tokens

- **Color system**: OKLCH color space for perceptually uniform colors
- **Primary**: `oklch(0.51 0.23 277)` (purple/violet)
- **Typography**: Inter (body), Geist Sans (headings), Geist Mono (code)
- **Border radius**: 10px base with calculated scale (6px-26px)
- **Component system**: shadcn/ui on @base-ui/react, CVA variants, Tailwind v4
- **Icons**: Lucide React
- **Density target**: Compact — small buttons (h-7 default), tight padding, xs-sm text sizes
